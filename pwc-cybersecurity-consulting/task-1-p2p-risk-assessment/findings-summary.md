# Findings Summary

## Client
MedTech Industries

## Engagement
Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Risk Assessment

## Business Process and Control Gaps Identified

1. Automatic approval of purchase requisitions
2. Lack of purchase order approval workflow
3. Absence of three-way match control
4. Shared General Ledger credentials
5. Excessive purchasing coordinator privileges
6. Single-signature cheque process
7. Weak user provisioning process
8. Privileged access granted through verbal requests

## Key Risks

- Unauthorized purchases
- Fraudulent transactions
- Excessive spending
- Unauthorized system access
- Segregation of duties violations
- Inadequate audit trails
- SOX compliance deficiencies

## Recommended Controls

Business Process Control Gaps:
- Require management approval before requisitions proceed.
- Implement a formal Purchase Order (PO) approval workflow.
- Require matching of Purchase Order, Goods Receipt, and Vendor Invoice before payment approval.
- Separate purchasing, vendor management, payment authorization, and check-signing responsibilities.
- Require dual authorization for payments above defined thresholds.

System Control Gaps:
- Implement unique user accounts and individual authentication.
- Establish a formal approval and access provisioning process.
- Require documented approvals and ticket-based access requests.

## Follow-Up Questions

1. Who approves purchase requisitions before procurement begins?
2. Is there a monetary threshold requiring management approval?
3. Is a three-way match performed before payment authorization?
4. Why are shared accounts used in the General Ledger system?
5. How frequently are user access reviews performed?
6. How are privileged accounts reviewed and monitored?
7. Are check signers independent from purchasing personnel?
8. Is there a formal access request and approval process?
9. Are vendor master file changes independently reviewed?
10. How are payment exceptions monitored and investigated?
