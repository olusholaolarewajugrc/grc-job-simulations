# SDLC Walkthrough Observations

## Positive Controls Observed

* Security considerations are integrated throughout the SDLC.
* Risk assessments are performed during development activities.
* Security testing is required before implementation.
* Formal authorization is required before systems enter production.
* Configuration management activities support change control.
* Continuous monitoring is emphasized during operations.
* Secure disposal procedures are incorporated into the lifecycle.

## Risks If Controls Are Not Followed

* Security vulnerabilities may be introduced into production systems.
* Unauthorized system changes may occur.
* Insufficient testing could result in system failures.
* Sensitive data may be exposed during system retirement.
* Regulatory and compliance requirements may not be met.

## Key Takeaways

The SDLC demonstrates how security should be embedded into every phase of system development rather than being added after deployment. Strong governance, risk assessment, testing, change management, and monitoring controls collectively reduce organizational risk.
